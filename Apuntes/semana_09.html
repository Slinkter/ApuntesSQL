📌 Objetivos: Agrupar datos, usar funciones de agregación, filtrar grupos con HAVING.

1. Funciones de Agregación

FunciónDescripciónEjemplo
COUNT(*)Total de filasCOUNT(*) = 100
SUM(campo)Suma de valoresSUM(salary) = 500000
AVG(campo)PromedioAVG(salary) = 50000
MIN(campo)Mínimo valorMIN(precio) = 10
MAX(campo)Máximo valorMAX(precio) = 1000

2. GROUP BY

-- Contar empleados por departamento
SELECT department, COUNT(*) as total
FROM employees
GROUP BY department;

-- Sumar salarios por departamento
SELECT department, SUM(salary) as masa_salarial
FROM employees
GROUP BY department;

-- Promedio de edad por ciudad
SELECT city, AVG(age) as edad_promedio
FROM employees
GROUP BY city;

3. HAVING - Filtrar Grupos

-- Departamentos con más de 5 empleados
SELECT department, COUNT(*) as total
FROM employees
GROUP BY department
HAVING COUNT(*) > 5;

-- Ciudades con promedio de salary > 50000
SELECT city, AVG(salary) as promedio
FROM employees
GROUP BY city
HAVING AVG(salary) > 50000;

Diferencia WHERE vs HAVING: WHERE filtra FILAS antes de agrupar. HAVing filtra GRUPOS después de GROUP BY.

4. ORDER BY con GROUP BY

-- Ordenar por cantidad (mayor a menor)
SELECT department, COUNT(*) as total
FROM employees
GROUP BY department
ORDER BY total DESC;

📝 Resumen: GROUP BY agrupa filas con valores iguales. Las funciones de agregación (COUNT, SUM, AVG, MIN, MAX) operan sobre cada grupo. HAVING filtra grupos, no filas individuales.

EJERCICIO

Mostrar departamento y promedio de salary de deptartamentos con promedio > 40000.

SELECT department, AVG(salary) as promedio
FROM employees
GROUP BY department
HAVING AVG(salary) > 40000;
